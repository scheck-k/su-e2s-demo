Listening samples for the paper:
Multi-speaker Speech Synthesis from Electromyographic Signals by Soft Speech Unit Prediction

Authors: Kevin Scheck, Tanja Schultz (Cognitive Systems Lab, University of Bremen, Germany)
Accepted at ICASSP 2023

Speech-Unit-based EMG-to-Speech

Conventional Electromyography-to-Speech (E2S) models convert Electromyography (EMG) signals to acoustic speech features, such as mel-spectrograms. In this work, we predict content representations of Voice Conversion (VC) models instead. For Speech-Unit-based EMG-to-Speech (SU-E2S), we predict soft speech units of the system of van Niekerk et al. (2022). We use the HuBERT-soft encoder to extract soft speech units of the acoustic speech target and predict soft units with an EMG encoder. We furthermore train a multi-speaker acoustic decoder, which processes soft speech units and a speaker embedding. As such, our model can perform multi-speaker E2S i.e. output the speech in a voice of another target speaker. We hope that we can leverage multi-speaker E2S for voice reconstruction in future work.

Listening examples

In the following, we present listening examples of the synthesis of a silent EMG signal to speech in multiple voices. The silent EMG signal is from the test set of the data set of Gaddy and Klein. The speakers for target voices are from the VCTK data set. Target speakers were either included during acoustic decoder and vocoder training (seen) or were never used in any training (unseen). The first listening example showcases the reference speech recording of the EMG speaker audibly saying the content of the silent EMG utterance.
